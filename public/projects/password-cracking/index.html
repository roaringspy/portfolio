Password Cracking Methodologies

Posted on Aug 27, 2025

A series of labs demonstrating proficiency in various password cracking techniques against different hashing algorithms and protocols.

The Objective

The goal was to gain hands-on experience with industry-standard password cracking tools and to understand the computational challenges and strategies involved in recovering passwords from captured hashes and live services.

The Process & Tools

I used a combination of tools within a controlled lab environment to perform both offline and online attacks:

  • Offline Attacks: Used John the Ripper and Hashcat to perform dictionary and brute-force attacks against password hashes (e.g., bcrypt). This involved leveraging wordlists and custom rule sets to efficiently crack the hashes.
  • Online Attacks: Used Hydra and the Metasploit Framework to simulate brute-force attacks against live services like FTP and SSH on a vulnerable target machine.

Key Learnings

This project solidified my understanding of secure password storage and the importance of implementing rate limiting and account lockout policies to defend against online brute-force attacks.
